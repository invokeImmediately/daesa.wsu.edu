Be an Engager

Make the most out of your college experience

Why should you partner with Center for Engaged Experiences (CEE) in taking ownership over your time at WSU? Because college should not only be educational but also transformative, preparing you to excel in your career, contribute meaningfully to your community, and succeed in taking on all of life's challenges. We provide the approach and guidance to ensure that this happens for you.

What will your portfolio contain?

When you work with us to take control over your own narrative at WSU, you'll have something to show for it. All of our graduates walk out of commencement with not only a diploma, but also a compelling online portfolio they can share with prospective employers.

It's all about the planning. Whether you are aware of it or not, every decision you make about how you spend your time at WSU will either help or hinder you in achieving your goals. You can be either reactive or proactive; choosing to be proactive is definitely the right way to go. Numerous lines of educational research have shown that good planning and solid time management practices are linked to performing better in college and graduating earlier. Moreover, following a well crafted plan as you navigate through WSU will help you accumulate experiences that will better prepare you to compete in the job market after you graduate.

Figuring out how to put together such a plan, and how to follow through with it, is where we come in. We help all undergraduate students—freshmen through seniors, regardless of major—achieve a maximally engaged, goal-fulfilling college experience by guiding them through these three steps:

Beginning your freshman year, you will start by meeting with us to begin discussing your career goals and how you can plan to engage in academic and extracurricular experiences that will help you achieve them. You will also sign up for Univ 499, a 3 credit course that is required for graduation and through which we will assist you in developing a formal four-year college engagement plan.

We will continue to work with you as you pursue your education at WSU and help you to refine and follow your four-year plan. As you meet with our peer mentors, we will assist you in utilizing effective marketing technologies to document your experiences and demonstrate how they align with your career goals.

To complete Univ 499, you will submit your portfolio for review no later than 1 month before commencement. You will not only earn three credits, but also graduate from WSU with a compelling online portfolio of college experiences that align with your career goals and which you can share with prospective employers or graduate schools.

Everybody goes to class—so let it be what you do outside of it that makes you stand out.

You are likely paying a lot for your education at WSU, so it only makes sense for you to seek the best value possible. What you may not be aware of is the wealth of experiences available to you at WSU that go beyond the classroom and can add so much more to your major and degree. That is why we help you develop and follow your own four-year plan. Putting it together makes you aware of all that WSU has to offer, and it ensures that you will have a roadmap to follow so that no opportunnity will be missed.

Shown below is a list of the kind of experiences that will go into your four-year plan. What's more, each item in the list is linked to a group of people at WSU that assists students in undergoing that type of experience.

  • Paid work
  • Internships (both for- and not-for-credit)
  • Undergraduate research
  • Awards (including scholarships, distinguished awards, plaques, public recogitions, etc.)
  • Certificate programs
  • Volunteering
  • Service learning
  • Foreign language and cultural competencies
  • Study abroad
  • Intramural and extramural sports
  • Clubs and hobbies
  • Leadership duties

Your four-year plan will be developed in the context of Univ 499, through which students who complete their four-year plan will receive 3 credits. Note that the course is required and completed prior to graduation, and that your portfolio materials must be submitted at least 1 month prior to commencement.

About Peer Mentoring

Our peer mentors are undergraduates who are experts in their own right at aligning their college experiences with their majors and future goals and knowing all about what WSU has to offer. They are juniors and seniors who have not only made significant progress on achieving their own four-year plan, but also have applied to be a peer mentor and been rigorously vetted and trained by our staff.
